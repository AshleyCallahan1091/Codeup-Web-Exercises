Zion National Park

  • Zion National Park has hiking trails that range from 30 minutes to four hours and range from beginning to experienced hiking skills. If it's your first time ever hiking, maybe you should just stick to the hipster rockwalls downtown.
  • Only experienced climbers should attempt climbing the sandstone cliffs and should only go from March-May or September-November because they'll get melted by the sun (not really but you'll look like a leathered lobster when you're done).
  • If you like horseback-riding and aren't afraid of a huge animal possibly throwing you off its back and trampling you when it gets spooked by a squirrel, feel free to schedule a horseback tour.
  • Canyoneering is a real activity that combines finding routes, rappelling, swimming, hiking and other things that will get you killed in places where rangers won't find you until dental records are needed in order to track down your worried-sick mother. Why would you do that to her? Stay safe. Play Firewatch instead.

Yellowstone National Park

  • Annual precipitation ranges from 10 inches at the north boundary to 80 inches in the southwest corner. So basically it goes from Texas rain levels normally to Texas rain levels in May 2016.
  • Yellowstone has an active volcano. Seriously, why would you even come here?
  • There are 290 waterfalls in Yellowstone. The only reasonable time to even consider jumping from a waterfall is if an Androminous-Rex is chasing you.
  • There are 67 species of mammals, all of which you should avoid.
